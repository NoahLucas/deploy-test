Motion Preview Route
Built to test a bolder animation system before we fold it into the main site
Noah Lucas

Motion that feels intentional, not decorative.

This route is a sandbox for ambient movement, reveal choreography, and depth so we can choose what belongs on the real page without guessing from static mockups.

3 modes Compare pacing and energy quickly.
Scroll aware Hero orb and cards respond to movement.
Accessible Reduced-motion fallback is built in.
What to look for The page should feel alive before interaction, then become more expressive as you scroll and switch intensity modes.
Production candidates Keep the staggered reveals, hero drift, and panel lift. Treat the orbital scene as a reference for tone, not a literal homepage element.
Animation Language

Small set of motions, clear purpose.

Reveal, drift, pulse, and parallax

Ambient Depth

Background washes move on a long loop so the surface never feels frozen. The motion stays broad and slow, which makes the page feel premium instead of busy.

background drift soft gradients

Staggered Entry

Cards enter with blur and vertical lift, offset just enough to create rhythm without turning the layout into a presentation deck.

stagger blur-to-sharp

Scroll Response

The hero orb and scene react to the viewport so there is a sense of elasticity tied to the user’s movement, not just autoplay loops.

parallax orb tracking

Mode Switching

The three motion modes help us judge whether the live site should feel restrained, cinematic, or more editorially charged.

calm cinematic charged

Hover Feedback

Cards lift slightly and sharpen their borders so interaction feels tactile without turning every element into a button.

panel lift shadow ramp

Reduced Motion

Every effect degrades cleanly when the system asks for it. That gives us room to be expressive without breaking accessibility discipline.

prefers-reduced-motion safe fallback
